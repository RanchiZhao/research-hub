# GSPO: Group Sequence Policy Optimization

## Problem: Granularity Mismatch in GRPO
- GRPO grants rewards at sequence level but computes ratios at token level
- This causes:
  - Ratio explosion for long sequences
  - High-variance gradients
  - MoE instability (expert-activation volatility)

## GSPO: Sequence-Level Importance Ratio
- w_GSPO(i) = [π_θ(y|x) / π_old(y|x)]^{1/|y|}
- Geometric mean of token ratios (length-normalized)
- Much tighter clipping range: [1-3e-4, 1+4e-4]
- One ratio per sequence, clipped as a whole

## Key Design Choices
- **Length normalization**: unifies numerical range across response lengths
- **Sequence-level clipping**: matches granularity of reward assignment
- Clips 100x more tokens than GRPO yet achieves better efficiency
  - Fewer but higher-quality gradient updates

## MoE Training Stability
- GRPO: expert-activation volatility → needs Routing Replay
- GSPO: focuses on sequence likelihood → inherently stable
- No Routing Replay needed, reduces memory overhead

## Results
- AIME'24: 85.7 (Qwen3-235B-A22B)
- Significantly higher training efficiency than GRPO
- Powers entire Qwen3 family

## Comparison
| Aspect | GRPO | GSPO |
|--------|------|------|
| IS ratio unit | Per token | Per sequence |
| Clip range | ~0.2 | ~3e-4 |
| MoE stability | Needs Routing Replay | Inherently stable |
